History of Astronomy


Early Astronomy China, 2136 BC, first to record a solar eclipse

Persia, 1100 AD, Omar Khayyan calculated a year to be 365.24219858156 days long. Accurate to the 6th decimal place

Greeks and Romans:

Pythagoras (around 500 BCE) a mathematician declared the universe is made of crystal spheres that encircle the Earth (geocentricity)

Aristotle (around 330 BCE): showed the Earth was spherical (eclipse) Thought the sun, planets, and stars were revolving around the Earth He showed scientifically the Earth could not be revolving around the Sun (stellar parallax)
