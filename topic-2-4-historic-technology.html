Automobiles replace horses

Here we are going to have some funny images of horses and poop and maybe horses dying and their carcasses lining the street. The automobile took away many chances of disease, allowed further travel in quicker time, but also added problems with our dependence on oil. We will create a graphic of this, but for now some key concepts the learner will gain from the graphics are below:
• The Automobile: Designed to replace the horse
• Environmental concerns of a horse in the city
• Each horse produces 15 to 35 pounds of manure and a quart of urine a day
• This contributed to the spread of infectious disease (flies as vectors)
• The disposal of horse carcasses: average city horse only lived 4 years-- in 1880 New York City had to remove 15,000 dead horses-- avg. weight 1,300 pounds
• Flaws: Our technology and our lifestyle now relies on private, petroleum-powered transportation
